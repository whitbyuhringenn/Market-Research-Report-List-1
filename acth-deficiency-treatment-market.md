<p><p>The ACTH deficiency treatment market is divided into two main types: congenital and acquired. Congenital ACTH deficiency occurs from genetic abnormalities leading to insufficient adrenocorticotropic hormone production at birth, typically requiring lifelong hormone replacement therapy. In contrast, acquired ACTH deficiency arises from external factors, such as pituitary tumors or traumatic brain injuries affecting hormone secretion. Treatments for acquired cases may involve addressing the underlying cause alongside hormone replacement. Both segments require targeted therapeutic strategies for effective management.</p></p>
